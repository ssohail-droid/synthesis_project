Message

In Fahrenheit 451, Ray Bradbury explores the consequences of a society that sacrifices knowledge and individual thought for conformity and comfort. Through Montag’s journey, the novel emphasizes that the suppression of literature and critical thinking leads to emotional emptiness, ignorance, and the loss of meaningful human experience.

Evidence

"A book is a loaded gun in the house next door. Burn it. Take the shot from the weapon." — Ray Bradbury

Montag's awakening through books shows how knowledge can spark personal freedom and resistance.
